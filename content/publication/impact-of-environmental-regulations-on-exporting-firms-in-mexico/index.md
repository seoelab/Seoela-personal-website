title: "Environmental Regulation, Firm Export Dynamics, and Welfare: Evidence
  from Mexico’s Air-Quality Programs"

abstract: "This paper studies how spatially targeted air-quality regulation
  affects firm emissions, export dynamics, and welfare in an open economy. I
  examine Mexico's ProAire programs, municipal and metropolitan air-quality
  plans, using the 2011-2020 rollout and linked firm data. A 2005-2018
  decomposition shows that Mexico's manufacturing emissions-intensity decline
  was driven mainly by within-industry technique changes rather than output
  reallocation. In the difference-in-differences analysis, ProAire is associated
  with a 24 percent decline in reported CO2 emissions among RETC-reporting
  firms. Export effects are concentrated on adjustment among existing exporters:
  entry changes little, exit rises by 2.7 percentage points, and continuing
  export sales fall by about 9 percent, with losses concentrated among smaller
  firms. A quantitative spatial model calibrated to national industry data
  measures the aggregate footprint of Mexico's environmental-regulation
  environment over the calibration period. It implies a 3.4 percent reduction in
  national emissions and a 0.85 percent welfare gain, with larger welfare gains
  under spatially targeted regulation than under an equal-abatement uniform
  policy."
